About Single Stories

A single story is a one sided point of view of something or someone.
Single stories have the power to tell false interpretations of the actual story.
The danger of a single story is that it creates stereotypes.

“The single story,” Chimamanda explained, “creates stereotypes, and the problem with stereotypes is not that they are untrue, but that they are incomplete. They make one story become the only story.”

Introduction

Many people think that all NFL players are from the United States because the National Football League has the lowest share of players born outside of the U.S.A. That is false, but the media doesn't do a good job of exposing the viewers to foreign born players. Think about it. Can you name an NFL player who is not American? Odds are you probably named a few or none at all.

How do foreign born players make it to the NFL?

1. By going to a high-school in the United States and going to a college to play American football and then becoming combine-eligible.
2. By being a great high-school American football player and earning an American college football scholarship and then becoming combine-eligible.
3. Through the International Player Pathway Program (IPPP), where if you are a) an elite/professional athlete and b) not American you may get an opportunity compete at the NFL level, improve your skills, and potentially earn a spot on an NFL roster.

Summary - You can be from any country in the world to be a National Football League (NFL) Player.

Why does the NFL want more foreign born players?

1. Identify NFL-ready talent around the world.
2. More foreign born players = More foreign born fans. Today the 10 countries with the most NFL fans other than the United States are Mexico, Brazil, Canada, South Korea, Germany, United Kingdom, Spain, Saudi Arabia, Australia, and Argentina.
3. Grow the game. "American football is for all. It’s a global game, an all-inclusive sport, that provides the opportunity for all ages to enjoy the values, fun and competitive environment unique to the sport of football,” said Troy Vincent, Sr., Executive Vice President of NFL Football Operations. “The NFL is committed to expanding NFL action to international fans in their home countries and developing young athletes around the world."

Conclusion: Not All NFL Players Are American

Just because you are American doesn't mean you will be a very good American football player. The only way to become successful, like in all sports from Ping Pong to Pro Basketball (NBA), is through desire and hard work.

What should I do?

Recognize that the NFL is made up of players from countries all over the world, not just the United States.
Understand that you don't have to be American or even live in the U.S. to be a National Football League (NFL) Player.
Help break the single story/stereotype by spreading the word to others.
