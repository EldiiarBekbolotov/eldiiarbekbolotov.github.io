About TCO

The Coding Olympics (TCO) is an unofficial annual summer coding competition on Khan Academy. It is usually held from June to August with around 5 challenges. It has been running since the summer of 2020 and has had over 750 programs submitted to be judged. This year, there are 3 teams with around 14 members per team.

Judging

All submissions will be judged on the following criteria: Complexity, Creativity, Design, Cleanliness, and Efficiency. For an in-depth description of the judging criteria, please visit the TCO judging criteria guide.

About This Site

Started: 19 June 2024
Completed: 22 June 2024
Estimated work time: 18 hours
Lines of code: 3000+
Languages: Vanilla HTML, CSS, JS

This website was programmed by me (LeaferStudios) as an entry to Khan Academy Computer Programming (KACP) Contest: Celebrations. I chose to revisit KACP Contest: Make Your Colors Count , which I didn't get to participate in because it was held before I created my account on Khan Academy. The colors I chose to use are Golden Yellow (#FFE100) because our mascot is a honey bee, as well as black (#000) and white (#FFF). Technically, I only used 2 colors since Evan Lewis said here that "any color with the same hue would be considered one color". I wanted to challenge myself and only use 1 shade of yellow.
This is also an entry to The Coding Olympics Homepage Challenge.
